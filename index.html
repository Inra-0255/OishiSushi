A Culinary Tradition

In Japan, sushi isn't just food—it's an art. I visited a small sushi bar in Shinjuku, where the chef greeted me with a smile and a bow. Watching him slice the fish with such precision was mesmerizing. Each piece of sushi was a masterpiece: melt-in-your-mouth toro (fatty tuna), fresh uni (sea urchin), and my favorite—salmon nigiri with a touch of yuzu.

The simplicity of ingredients like soy sauce, mirin, and dashi created deep,
rich tastes that stayed with me long after each meal.
